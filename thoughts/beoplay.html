BeoPlay 8E wireless earphones UI: first impressions

Bang&Olufsen E-8 wireless earbuds is something I have carried around for a year now, my private entertainment “on the go”, which with the charging box can last up to 16 hours playtime.



There were my first wireless earphones and using them while biking, running or just stitting in a park is just purely effortless, so much so I would sometimes even forgethaving them in my ears.

The sound output quality is easily adjustable through Bang&Olufsen Beosonic app, which allows users to tailor their bespoke music experience through a simplistic and trully intuitive UI. The nomination of the app for UX awards 2020 was if not more than expected then at least well deserved.

However, this time I wanted to talk about controlling the earphones locally. Both earphones have a touchpad, which records tap gestures and allows me to intuitively switch between tracks, take calls, and activate transparency mode and voice commands.

To be completely honest, my first days of using this product, the gestures did not seem intuitive or natural at all and I was just constantly switing through tracks, switing to transparency mode and so on.

The touchpad is active from the moment it’s taken out of the charging box until the moment is but back in. Therefore, for the first days I had to be very careful not to touch them on accident, because I would have to spend some time touching the side of the earphone to figure out what I did wrong.

The hardware that the earphones use for the touch IU was not noted anywhere on the Starting guide, User Manual or Safety Information manual. However, with some thought to it and additional research on other earplugs and touchpads, I would speculate that the touch UI operates with a tiny capacitive touchpad. Capacitive sensor reads the electric field around it and detects changes, whether an object (such as human finger), approaches the sensor and breaks the electric field. This change is then measured and interpreted by the device itself, allowing it to be controlled through touch-gesture interfaces.

Is this the best possible way of controlling these earplugs? Definitely, because they are great once you learn to operate them, however learning takes time due to lack of information and it might even become frusatrating for the user in the beginning. It could be easily fixed with designing a touch system with a higher treshold for registering touch as input or simply creating a more user-friendly and welcoming onboarding process for the first time of usage os the product.
