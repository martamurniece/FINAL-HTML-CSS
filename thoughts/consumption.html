UX & sustainability?

What can we do as UX designers?

Materiality of the Internet is becoming a more and more discussed topic nowadays and the answer to whose responsibility it is, unfurtunately is still unclear. Should the tech industry take the lead into solving the energy consumption by digital devices or should it be us - the consumers and their demands?



Internet sustainablility question finds daylight more and more often as it keeps keeps evolving and reaches the next generation of Internet - Internet of Things. Sources speculate that by 2030 30% of global energy consumption will be used to maintain the Internet and promising that by 2040 this will be completely unsustainable, therefore implying that the solutions and designs we're using today already painted a dark futuristic picture for our society yesterday.

Let’s imagine a scenario that all our technology is powered from unsustainable energy sources. If tomorrow we wake up and realize that digital products will have a quota on energy consumption, the world will have to drastically change. Most of our devices could not be used anymore at all, or their usage would be limited to a couple of times per day, therore strongly changing our digital everyday habits from working to leisure activities.

First, we would have to focus all our attention into understanding how much energy exactly does each bit of digital and technological development demand. How much data does a video need to load in a page? What about the data that is stored about the user and never used? ( We are afterall only using 6% of the stored data in data centers.) What about the fact that as hard as we’ll try we wont get to the end of your feed on Facebook ? Unfurtunately, our devices are build to keep us engaged by generating personally tailored content as much as possible.

We would have to limit the functionalities of devices and leave just the most necessary functionalities, we would have to make a lot of extremely important and radically innovative design decisions choosing between user online habits and energy consumption.

The ecologies of the cloud are not built to be sustainable, but they are adaptable. I think what we have to realize as UX designers is that designing sustainably was our responsibility already yesterday, so today we will have to design for yesterday and today. The faster we learn to do that, the bigger and more positive impact on the future we will have.

We already have the knowledge of methods for user centered design. I am wondering if we could implement this knowledge to design for sustainability? For example, what about drawing system-diagrams and recognizing the unsustainable parts? Maybe, should we map out an “energy-journey” for the power used in our products? Let’s think and apply a little creativity to find out!
